────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Reactivation of Oedipal conflicts from Phallic stage, combined with new genital awareness. Unsolved stage conflicts surfacing.

Both Freud and Aristotle: Adolescence critical period for developing character. Freud: sexual identity; Aristotle: virtue development

Humans are social beings. Connection = essential for eudaimonia. Isolation = inability to flourish

Q6. PRIMARY TOPIC: Defence Mechanisms
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:
How can PSW differentiate between adaptive sublimation and maladaptive acting out in same behavior (exercise)?

ANSWER:
Sublimation: Person feels better, relationships improve, achievement continues. Acting out: Person injuries self, relationships suffer, no progress

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:
Connects to: Normal vs Abnormal, Therapeutic assessment, Mental Health outcomes

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:
Both involve physical activity (exercise). Virtue ethics: Context and intention matter. Sublimation = virtuous; Acting out = avoidance

Q7. PRIMARY TOPIC: Normal vs Abnormal Behavior
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:
A man hears voices telling him to help others. He voluntarily works in hospital. Is this abnormal?

ANSWER:
Biopsychosocial assessment needed: If hallucinations cause distress/dysfunction, abnormal. If not distressing and he's functioning, may be spiritual experience

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:
Connects to: Mental Health definition, Cultural differences, Psychological assessment

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:
Context matters. Aristotle: virtue is contextual. Hearing voices = could be spiritual gift (virtue) or illness depending on context

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:
You have a client you personally dislike. How do you apply "Principle of Acceptance"?

Separate person from behavior. Recognize your counter-transference. Maintain professional standards despite personal feelings. Reflect on bias.

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:
Connects to: Counter-transference management, Professional ethics, Therapeutic relationship

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:
Virtue of Justice: Fair treatment regardless of personal preference. Practical wisdom to manage own emotions professionally

Q10. PRIMARY TOPIC: Social Welfare Concepts
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:
Why did Beveridge Report emphasize systemic welfare over charity? How does this relate to social justice?

ANSWER:
Charity = discretionary, inconsistent, stigmatizing. Welfare = right-based, systematic, universal. Reflects justice principle of fairness.

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:
Connects to: Social Work values, Professional ethics, Government responsibility

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:
Aristotle's distributive justice: Fair and systematic distribution. Welfare system = implementation of justice principle
