
# AIIMS CRE Psychiatric Social Worker Exam - Practice Questions

This document contains practice questions for the subject knowledge section of the AIIMS CRE Psychiatric Social Worker Exam. The questions are categorized by the topics listed in the syllabus.

## Social Welfare and Social Work

**Question 1:** What is the primary goal of social work?
A. To provide therapy and counseling services.
B. To advocate for social justice and equality.
C. To administer government programs and policies.
D. To conduct research on social issues.

**Answer:** B. To advocate for social justice and equality.

**Question 2:** What is the person-in-environment perspective in social work?
A. The understanding that individuals are influenced by their social and physical environments.
B. The focus on individual thoughts and feelings in therapy sessions.
C. The belief that individuals are solely responsible for their own well-being.
D. The emphasis on personal growth and self-actualization.

**Answer:** A. The understanding that individuals are influenced by their social and physical environments.

**Question 3:** Which of the following is an example of micro-level social work practice?
A. Community organizing and development.
B. Policy analysis and advocacy.
C. Individual counseling and therapy.
D. Program evaluation and research.

**Answer:** C. Individual counseling and therapy.

**Question 4:** What is the purpose of the social work code of ethics?
A. To provide guidelines for ethical decision-making and professional conduct.
B. To regulate and license social work practitioners.
C. To ensure compliance with government regulations and policies.
D. To conduct research on social issues.

**Answer:** A. To provide guidelines for ethical decision-making and professional conduct.

**Question 5:** What is the role of a social worker in case management?
A. To diagnose and treat mental health disorders.
B. To provide financial assistance and resources to individuals and families.
C. To coordinate and monitor services to meet the needs of clients.
D. To conduct research and evaluation studies on social issues.

**Answer:** C. To coordinate and monitor services to meet the needs of clients.

## Professional Social Work

**Question 1:** A therapist is meeting with a client for an initial evaluation. The client reports feeling moody and has been having many headaches. The client states that she lost a lot of weight recently and has noticed a decrease in appetite. The clinician should FIRST:
A. Assess the client for suicidality.
B. Refer the client to a psychiatrist.
C. Complete a mental status exam.
D. Refer the client to a Medical Doctor (MD).

**Answer:** D. Refer the client to a MD.

**Question 2:** A social worker in a psychiatric setting is working with a client who exhibits characteristics of Bipolar I Disorder. The psychiatrist in the clinic plans to prescribe medication for the client. Which of the following is the most commonly used medication for Bipolar I Disorder?
A. Lithium
B. Ritalin
C. Zoloft
D. Prozac

**Answer:** A. Lithium.

**Question 3:** During Mrs. S's first appointment in a family agency, the social worker encourages her to express her feelings about the recent placement of her child in a residential facility for the developmentally disabled. Mrs. S talks at length instead about her physical health problems. The social worker should:
A. use Mrs. S's remarks about her health to identify unresolved parenting issues.
B. refocus the interview on the child's behavior and the reason for the residential placement.
C. explore with Mrs. S how her health problems are related to the recent placement of her child.
D. listen to Mrs. S's description of her health as a beginning of a relationship and means of assessing her self-perception.

**Answer:** D. listen to Mrs. S's description of her health as a beginning of a relationship and means of assessing her self-perception.

## Psychology & Mental Health

**Question 1:** A college student has made an appointment to speak with a counselor at her university's counseling center about troublesome symptoms she has experienced on a number of occasions over the last six months. The symptoms include chest pain, nausea, pounding heart, and dizziness. A recent medical exam failed to identify any physical problems. Which of the following DSM-5 diagnoses is this student MOST LIKELY suffering from?
A) Generalized Anxiety Disorder
B) Social Anxiety Disorder
C) Panic Disorder
D) Specific Phobia

**Answer:** C) Panic Disorder.

**Question 2:** What is the primary role of a psychiatric social worker?
A) Conducting psychological assessments
B) Providing mental health services to individuals and families
C) Prescribing medication
D) Performing surgical procedures

**Answer:** B) Providing mental health services to individuals and families.

**Question 3:** What is the difference between delusions and hallucinations when a social worker is making an assessment with a client presenting with psychiatric issues?
A. Delusions are false, fixed beliefs despite evidence to the contrary, while hallucinations are hearing, seeing, smelling, or feeling something that is not real.
B. Hallucinations are false, fixed beliefs despite evidence to the contrary, while delusions are hearing, seeing, smelling, or feeling something that is not real.
C. Delusions and hallucinations are both types of thought disorders.
D. Delusions are always persecutory, while hallucinations are always auditory.

**Answer:** A. Delusions are false, fixed beliefs despite evidence to the contrary, while hallucinations are hearing, seeing, smelling, or feeling something that is not real.

## Sociology

**Question 1:** Which sociological perspective emphasizes the role of power, inequality, and conflict in shaping society and mental health outcomes?
a) Functionalism
b) Symbolic Interactionism
c) Conflict Theory
d) Social Exchange Theory

**Answer:** c) Conflict Theory

**Question 2:** The concept of "stigma" in sociology, particularly concerning mental illness, refers to:
a) A biological marker indicating a predisposition to mental illness.
b) A deeply discrediting attribute that reduces a person from a whole and usual person to a tainted, discounted one.
c) A legal term used to describe individuals deemed incompetent due to mental health issues.
d) A therapeutic technique used to challenge negative thought patterns.

**Answer:** b) A deeply discrediting attribute that reduces a person from a whole and usual person to a tainted, discounted one.

**Question 3:** Emile Durkheim's study of suicide highlighted the importance of which sociological factor?
a) Economic recession
b) Individual psychological disorders
c) Social integration and regulation
d) Genetic inheritance

**Answer:** c) Social integration and regulation

## Social Psychology

**Question 1:**
Which of the following best describes the concept of "social loafing"?
A) An individual's tendency to exert less effort when working in a group than when working alone.
B) The enhancement of a group's prevailing inclinations through discussion within the group.
C) The loss of self-awareness and self-restraint occurring in group situations that foster arousal and anonymity.
D) A mode of thinking that persons engage in when concurrence-seeking becomes so dominant in a cohesive in-group that it tends to override realistic appraisal of alternative courses of action.

**Answer:** A) An individual's tendency to exert less effort when working in a group than when working alone.

**Question 2:**
The "fundamental attribution error" refers to the tendency for observers to:
A) Underestimate the impact of situational factors and overestimate the impact of dispositional factors when explaining others' behavior.
B) Overestimate the impact of situational factors and underestimate the impact of dispositional factors when explaining others' behavior.
C) Attribute their own successes to internal factors and their failures to external factors.
D) Attribute others' successes to external factors and their failures to internal factors.

**Answer:** A) Underestimate the impact of situational factors and overestimate the impact of dispositional factors when explaining others' behavior.

## Relevant Welfare Legislation and Contemporary Issues

**Question 1:** Which Act replaced the Mental Health Act of 1987 in India, aiming to provide mental healthcare services and protect the rights of persons with mental illness?
A. Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016
B. National Trust Act, 1999
C. Mental Healthcare Act, 2017
D. Persons with Disabilities Act, 1995

**Correct Answer:** C

**Question 2:** The Mental Healthcare Act, 2017, decriminalized which of the following acts, presuming the person to be suffering from mental illness at that time?
A. Public nuisance
B. Attempt to suicide
C. Drug abuse
D. Theft

**Correct Answer:** B

**Question 3:** What is the primary objective of the National Mental Health Programme (NMHP) launched in India in 1982?
A. To establish private mental health institutions across the country.
B. To ensure the availability and accessibility of minimum mental healthcare for all, particularly vulnerable sections.
C. To focus solely on pharmacological treatments for mental illnesses.
D. To promote institutionalization of persons with mental illness.

**Correct Answer:** B
