## **Detailed Explanations**

*(Explanations for each question would be provided here, referencing the concepts from the previous chapters. For brevity in this format, I will provide explanations for a few key questions.)*

*   **1. (B) Foot:** A glove is worn on a hand; a shoe is worn on a foot. (Analogy of function).
*   **11. (B) ₹200:** The book was sold at 75% of its original price (100% - 25%). So, if 75% = ₹150, then 1% = ₹2. Therefore, 100% = ₹200.
*   **16. (B) have submitted:** The subject is "Each" (Each of the students), which is singular. Therefore, the verb should be singular: "has submitted".
*   **21. (B) Social Casework:** This is the definition of casework, the one-to-one method.
*   **22. (B) Ego:** The Ego mediates between the Id's desires and reality's constraints.
*   **23. (C) Ascribed status:** A status assigned at birth.
*   **24. (C) Adolescence:** This is the central crisis of the teenage years.
*   **27. (A) Primary group:** A family is the classic example of a small, intimate, long-term group.
*   **29. (B) Assimilation:** The process of a minority group being absorbed into the dominant culture.
*   **31. (B) Obedience:** Milgram's shock experiment was a study of obedience to authority.
*   **32. (D) Self-Determination:** This principle respects the client's right to make their own choices.
*   **35. (B) Prejudice:** Prejudice is a negative feeling/attitude. Discrimination is the action.
*   **38. (B) Counter-transference:** The worker is projecting their own feelings onto the client.
*   **43. (B) Social Assistance:** Assistance is needs-based and tax-funded. Insurance is contribution-based.
*   **47. (B) Role Conflict:** The conflict is between the roles of two different statuses (employee and mother). Role strain is tension within a single status.
*   **49. (A) Mary Richmond:** Her book laid the foundation for professional social casework.
