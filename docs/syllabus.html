Detailed Syllabus for AIIMS CRE Psychiatric Social Worker Exam

The AIIMS CRE exam for Psychiatric Social Worker (also including Medical Social Worker posts in some institutes) is organized into two major components: General Section (General Intelligence/Reasoning, General Awareness, English) and Subject Knowledge (Professional Social Work, Psychology, Sociology, Mental Health). Each section has specific key topics you need to cover:

***

A. General Intelligence and Reasoning (20 Marks)

  • **Analogies**

Recognizing relationships, similarities, and differences between pairs of elements.

  • **Similarities & Differences**

Identification of common/different features between items or concepts.

  • **Space Visualization**

Understanding visual patterns, shapes, and spatial forms.

  • **Problem Solving, Analysis, Judgment, Decision Making**

Deductive and logical reasoning, evaluating situations, drawing sound conclusions.

  • **Visual Memory, Discriminating Observation**

Recall visual sequences, recognize patterns quickly.

  • **Relationship Concepts**

Problems relating to family, blood relations, and others.

  • **Arithmetical Reasoning, Number Series & Non-Verbal Series**

Basic logic, pattern detection.

  • **Classification, Coding-Decoding, Directions, Venn Diagrams, Statement & Conclusion, Puzzles**

***

B. English Language (20 Marks)

  • **Basics of English Grammar & Usage**

Tenses, Prepositions, Articles, Syntax.

  • **Spot the Error / Fill in the Blanks**
  • **Synonyms/Homonyms, Antonyms**
  • **Spellings / Detecting Mis-spelt Words**
  • **Idioms & Phrases; One-Word Substitution**
  • **Improvement of Sentences**
  • **Active/Passive Voice; Direct/Indirect Narration**
  • **Shuffling of Sentence Parts**, Ordering of sentences in a passage
  • **Cloze Passage & Comprehension Passage**

***

C. Subject Knowledge (Professional & Psychiatric Social Work, Mental Health, Sociology, Psychology) (60 Marks)

1. Social Welfare and Social Work

  • **Concept, Need, and Objectives of Social Welfare**
  • **Philosophy & History of Social Welfare** (UK, USA, India)
  • **Social Development, Social Planning, Social Reform, Social Security, Social Justice, Social Policy, Social Legislation**
  • **Human Rights**

History, constitutional provisions, and relevant acts

  • **Social and Welfare Services**, Welfare for SC/ST/OBC, children, women, aged, disabled.

2. Professional Social Work

  • **Concept, Nature, & Characteristics of Professional Social Work**
  • **Principles, Values, Ethics of Social Work**
  • **Professional Status of Social Work in India, Code of Ethics**
  • **Methods of Social Work** (Primary: Case work, Group work, Community organization; Secondary: Social Action, Social Welfare Administration, Social Work Research)
  • **Interface between Professional and Voluntary Social Work**

3. Psychology & Mental Health

  • **Definition, Fields, and Scope of Psychology**
  • **Personality Theories**:
  • Freud’s Psychoanalytic Theory (Psycho-sexual stages, Structure of mind)
  • Erikson’s Psychosocial Development
  • Defense Mechanisms
  • **Human Development**:
  • Stages (Prenatal to Old Age)
  • Heredity and Environment
  • Mental Hygiene: Aims, principles, techniques, and mental health promotion
  • **Psychopathology and Abnormal Psychology**

Basic concepts, major disorders, diagnostic criteria

4. Sociology

  • **Nature and Scope of Sociology**
  • **Theories of Society**: Evolutionary, Cyclical, Conflict, Systems
  • **Social Groups**: Primary, Secondary, Reference Groups
  • **Social Institutions**: Family, Marriage, Kinship, Property (trends in India)
  • **Culture, Socialization, Traditions, Values, Norms**
  • **Social System & Sub-System**: Structure, Function
  • **Social Process**: Social Interaction, Cooperation, Conflict, Assimilation, Social Control

5. Social Psychology

  • **Nature and Scope of Social Psychology**
  • **Attitude**: Formation, Measurement, Prejudice, Discrimination
  • **Communication**: Concepts, Barriers, Methods, Mass Communication
  • **Public Opinion, Propaganda, Fashion, Social Facilitation, Crowd Behavior**

6. Relevant Welfare Legislation and Contemporary Issues

  • **Key Social Legislations**: Acts relating to welfare of children, women, elderly, disabled, SC/ST/OBC, mental health
  • **Welfare Programs/Schemes**: Major central/state government schemes for vulnerable sections
  • **Current Trends and Recent Developments**: Mental health care act, social inclusion, disability rights, geriatric social work, etc.

***

Note:

  • The technical/subject section of the exam generally includes a mix of direct theory-based questions, clinical scenario/case-based application, matching, and assertion-reason style questions.
  • Difficulty level corresponds to post-graduate level (MA/MSW/M.Phil. standard) for subject content and undergraduate (BA/B.Sc./B.Com) level for General and English sections.[1][2][3][4][5][6]

It is advisable to also consult the indicative syllabus issued in recruitment advertisements and official AIIMS notifications, as institutes may vary slightly in emphasis.[2][6][7][1]

For further topic lists or micro-syllabus breakdown (e.g., detailed acts, model development questions) or references to standard textbooks and notes, request specifics by sub-topic/section.
