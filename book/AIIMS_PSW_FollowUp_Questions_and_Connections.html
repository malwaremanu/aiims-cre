Q1. PRIMARY TOPIC: Freud Psychosexual Development

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

If a person has Oral Fixation showing dependency, what would be their likely defence mechanism?

ANSWER:

Projection (blaming others for their neediness) or Displacement (seeking comfort from unhealthy sources)

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Defence Mechanisms, Normal vs Abnormal, Therapeutic Intervention

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Virtue of temperance: Seeking pleasure in moderation. Fixation = excess gratification = vice

Q2. PRIMARY TOPIC: Freud Psychosexual Development

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

How would Freud explain adolescent mood swings and identity confusion?

ANSWER:

Reactivation of Oedipal conflicts from Phallic stage, combined with new genital awareness. Unsolved stage conflicts surfacing.

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Erikson Stage 5 (Identity vs Confusion), Abnormal vs Normal behavior, Mental Health

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Both Freud and Aristotle: Adolescence critical period for developing character. Freud: sexual identity; Aristotle: virtue development

Q3. PRIMARY TOPIC: Erikson Psychosocial Development

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

A 70-year-old client comes to PSW saying "My life was a waste; I never achieved anything." Which stage is unresolved?

ANSWER:

Stage 7: Generativity vs Stagnation - Did not feel they contributed meaningfully; now experiencing despair in Stage 8

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Depression in elderly, Social Support importance, Therapeutic counseling approach

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Aristotle: Eudaimonia requires meaningful contribution. Client lacking this = unable to flourish

Q4. PRIMARY TOPIC: Mental Health & Mental Hygiene

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

Why does social support improve mental health in depression? Connect to multiple theories.

ANSWER:

Trust development (Erikson Stage 1), reduces isolation, provides belonging need (Maslow), activates mature defence (altruism)

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Erikson, Maslow, Defence Mechanisms, Social Psychology (relationships)

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Humans are social beings. Connection = essential for eudaimonia. Isolation = inability to flourish

====================================================================================================

Q5. PRIMARY TOPIC: Social Work Methods

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

For treating child sexual abuse victim, why might Group Work be better than only Case Work?

ANSWER:

Group provides: Normalization ("I'm not alone"), Peer support, Modeling healthy responses, Reduced shame through mutual support

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Social Support, Defence Mechanisms (shared trauma), Therapeutic relationship

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Practical wisdom: Choose method fitting client need. Group work shows prudence in understanding healing needs

Q6. PRIMARY TOPIC: Defence Mechanisms

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

How can PSW differentiate between adaptive sublimation and maladaptive acting out in same behavior (exercise)?

ANSWER:

Sublimation: Person feels better, relationships improve, achievement continues. Acting out: Person injuries self, relationships suffer, no progress

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Normal vs Abnormal, Therapeutic assessment, Mental Health outcomes

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Both involve physical activity (exercise). Virtue ethics: Context and intention matter. Sublimation = virtuous; Acting out = avoidance

Q7. PRIMARY TOPIC: Normal vs Abnormal Behavior

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

A man hears voices telling him to help others. He voluntarily works in hospital. Is this abnormal?

ANSWER:

Biopsychosocial assessment needed: If hallucinations cause distress/dysfunction, abnormal. If not distressing and he's functioning, may be spiritual experience

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Mental Health definition, Cultural differences, Psychological assessment

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Context matters. Aristotle: virtue is contextual. Hearing voices = could be spiritual gift (virtue) or illness depending on context

Q8. PRIMARY TOPIC: Social Work Principles & Values

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

You have a client you personally dislike. How do you apply "Principle of Acceptance"?

ANSWER:

Separate person from behavior. Recognize your counter-transference. Maintain professional standards despite personal feelings. Reflect on bias.

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Counter-transference management, Professional ethics, Therapeutic relationship

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Virtue of Justice: Fair treatment regardless of personal preference. Practical wisdom to manage own emotions professionally

Q9. PRIMARY TOPIC: Therapeutic Concepts

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

Client becomes angry at you for "not understanding." But you've been empathetic. What's likely happening?

ANSWER:

Transference: Client's past experience of misunderstanding (parent, authority figure) projected onto you. Use therapeutically to explore pattern.

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Personality development (root in past), Therapeutic relationship building, Client insights

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Practical wisdom: Recognize that anger isn't about you personally. Help client develop self-knowledge (virtue of humility)

Q10. PRIMARY TOPIC: Social Welfare Concepts

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

Why did Beveridge Report emphasize systemic welfare over charity? How does this relate to social justice?

ANSWER:

Charity = discretionary, inconsistent, stigmatizing. Welfare = right-based, systematic, universal. Reflects justice principle of fairness.

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Social Work values, Professional ethics, Government responsibility

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Aristotle's distributive justice: Fair and systematic distribution. Welfare system = implementation of justice principle

Q11. PRIMARY TOPIC: Sociology: Social Institutions

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

How do family kinship systems affect mental health and social support in Erikson's framework?

ANSWER:

Extended kinship provides: Trust (Stage 1), Autonomy support (Stage 2), Industry opportunities (Stage 4), Intimate relationships (Stage 6)

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

Connects to: Erikson stages, Mental Health, Social Support importance

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Family as natural community. Kinship systems organized to develop human flourishing through stages

Q12. PRIMARY TOPIC: Cross-Topic Integration

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

A woman has been abused as child (trauma), has poor self-esteem (normal in abuse), uses alcohol to cope (acting out). Is she abnormal?

ANSWER:

Biopsychosocial: Normal response to abnormal situation. Trauma (biological/psychological damage), learned coping (defence mechanism), social consequences

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

ALL TOPICS: Combines trauma history, coping mechanisms, social context, therapeutic intervention needed

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Person capable of virtue but circumstances prevent flourishing. Therapy goal: Restore capacity to achieve eudaimonia

Q13. PRIMARY TOPIC: Cross-Topic Integration

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

Design intervention for adolescent with identity confusion, poor family relationships, and isolation. Which methods/principles apply?

ANSWER:

Casework (assess history), Group work (peer support), Community org (family/school involvement). Principles: Acceptance, self-determination, empowerment

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

ALL TOPICS: Erikson (Stage 5), Social work methods, Principles, Mental Health

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Practical wisdom in selecting integrated approach. Goal: Help adolescent develop identity and virtue

Q14. PRIMARY TOPIC: ARISTOTLE - Central Concept

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

FOLLOW-UP QUESTION:

How do AIIMS PSW exam topics connect to Aristotle's philosophy of human flourishing (Eudaimonia)?

ANSWER:

Personality development (Freud/Erikson) = path to character. Social institutions (family) = context. Mental health = capacity to flourish.

TOPIC CONNECTIONS:

OVERARCHING: All topics are about helping humans achieve eudaimonia - their full potential

ARISTOTLE CONNECTION:

Core: Virtue ethics. Virtues developed through: healthy relationships (family), resolving crises (Erikson), managing emotions (defence mechanisms), ethical practice (principles)
