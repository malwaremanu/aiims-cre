    AIIMS CRE Psychiatric Social Worker: Complete Preparation Guide

    Exam Pattern & Syllabus

    The AIIMS Common Recruitment Examination (CRE) for Psychiatric Social Worker consists of 100 Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs) worth 400 marks, with a 90-minute duration. The exam follows this pattern:[1]

    Section-wise Distribution: - General Section (25 questions, 100 marks): General Intelligence & Reasoning (20 marks) and English Language (20 marks)[2] - Subject Knowledge (75 questions, 300 marks): Social Work and Psychology domain-specific content[2]

    Marking Scheme: - Correct Answer: +4 marks - Wrong Answer: -1 mark (25% negative marking) - Exam Language: English only (for technical/graduate posts)[3]

    Detailed Syllabus

    A. General Intelligence and Reasoning (20 Marks)

    Analogies, similarities and differences, space visualization, problem solving, analysis, judgment, decision making, visual memory, discriminating observation, relationship concepts, arithmetical reasoning, verbal and figure classification, arithmetical number series, non-verbal series[2]

    B. English Language (20 Marks)

    Spot the Error, Fill in the Blanks, Synonyms/Homonyms, Antonyms, Spellings, Idioms & Phrases, One word substitution, Improvement of Sentences, Active/Passive Voice, Direct/Indirect narration, Shuffling of Sentence parts, Cloze Passage, Comprehension Passage[2]

    C. Subject Knowledge (50 Marks) - Core Topics

    1. Framework: Social Welfare - Concept, need and objectives of Social Welfare - Philosophy of Social Welfare and Social work - Social welfare in historical perspective - Changing concepts and practices of social welfare - Social Welfare and related terms: Social Development, Social Planning, Social Reform, Social Security, Social Policy, Social Action, Social Justice, Social and welfare services, Social legislation, Human Rights[2]

    2. Professional Social Work - Concept of professional social work - Evolution in UK, USA, and India - Basic principles and values - Nature and characteristics of a profession - Professional status of Social work in India - Code of ethics for social workers - Methods of Social Work: Primary and Secondary methods - Integrated approach and Interface between Professional and voluntary social work[2]

    3. Psychology and Mental Health: Fundamentals - Psychology: Definitions and Fields - Mental Health: Meaning, Definitions, Characteristics - Normal & Abnormal Behaviour: Meaning, Characteristics[2]

    4. Human Development - Heredity and Environment - Mental Hygiene: Definition, scope, Characteristics, Importance, Aims, Principles, Programme - Developmental Stages: Prenatal, Infancy, Babyhood, Childhood, Puberty, Adolescence, Adulthood, Middle age, Old age[2]

    5. Personality Development Theories - Psycho-Sexual development theory: Sigmund Freud - Psychosocial development theory: Erik Erikson - Defence Mechanisms - Perspectives of Psychopathology[2]

    6. Social Psychology - Nature and scope of social psychology - Attitude: nature and measurement, prejudice and discrimination - Communication: concept, methods, skills, major obstacles - Mass communication, public opinion, propaganda, fashion, social facilitation, crowd behaviour[2]

    7. Sociology: Theoretical Perspective - Society: Nature, Approaches, Functions, Theories (Evolutionary, Cyclical, Conflict, Systems) - Social Group: Primary, Secondary, Reference Groups - Social Institutions: Family, Marriage, Kinship, Property (Present trends) - Culture: Traditions, Customs, Values and Norms - Social System & Sub-system: Structure & Function - Social Structures: Status & Role - Social Process: Social Interaction, Socialization, Cooperation, Conflict, Assimilation, Social control[2]

    Important Topics (High Weightage)

    Priority 1 - Must Cover (40-50% weightage): 1. Professional Social Work: Principles, values, methods, code of ethics 2. Personality Theories: Freud's Psycho-Sexual Development, Erikson's Psychosocial Development 3. Mental Health: Concepts, characteristics, mental hygiene principles 4. Human Development: All developmental stages from prenatal to old age 5. Social Psychology: Attitude formation, Communication process, prejudice 6. Defence Mechanisms: All major types and examples[12][11][2]

    Priority 2 - Important Topics (30-35% weightage): 1. Social Welfare: Evolution, concepts, and related terms 2. Sociology: Social institutions (Family, Marriage, Kinship) 3. Social System and Social Process 4. Normal vs. Abnormal Behavior 5. Social Work Methods (Primary and Secondary) 6. Perspectives of Psychopathology[2]

    Priority 3 - Moderate Weightage (15-20% weightage): 1. History of Social Work in India, UK, USA 2. Culture, Traditions, Values and Norms 3. Social structures (Status and Role) 4. Heredity and Environment 5. Social legislation and Human Rights[2]

    Detailed 1-Month Study Plan

    Study Plan Highlights:

    Week 1: Foundation Building - Days 1-2: General Intelligence basics + English fundamentals + Social Welfare concepts - Days 3-4: Reasoning practice + Professional Social Work + Psychology basics - Days 5-6: English comprehension + Human Development + Personality theories - Day 7: Weekly revision + Mock test (General section)

    Week 2: Core Psychology & Social Work - Days 8-9: Developmental stages + Defence mechanisms + Social Psychology - Days 10-11: Sociology concepts + Social institutions + Social structures - Days 12-13: Current affairs + Social Work methods + Professional ethics - Day 14: Weekly revision + Subject knowledge mock test

    Week 3: Advanced Topics & Integration - Days 15-16: Advanced English + Mental Hygiene + Social Systems - Days 17-18: Reasoning practice + Social legislation + Social processes - Days 19-20: Current affairs updates + Social Policy + Full paper practice - Day 21: Full-length mock test + Comprehensive review

    Week 4: Intensive Practice & Final Revision - Days 22-23: High-weightage topics revision + Practice questions (80+) - Days 24-25: Previous year papers analysis + Frequently asked questions - Days 26-27: Weak areas practice + Subject-wise notes review + Mock test - Days 28-29: Final revision + Mock test series (2 tests) + Time management - Day 30: Light revision + Exam strategy + Mental preparation

    Daily Schedule (9 hours study): - Morning (9 AM - 12 PM): New topic study or General section - Afternoon (2 PM - 5 PM): Subject knowledge or Conceptual topics - Evening (6 PM - 9 PM): Practice, revision, or mixed topics - Night: Review notes, solve 20-30 practice questions

    Preparation Strategy & Tips

    1. Study Approach: - Start with NCERT books for Psychology and Sociology basics - Use standard Social Work textbooks (MA/MSc level) - Create concise notes for quick revision - Focus on understanding concepts rather than rote learning[13][1]

    2. Practice Strategy: - Solve at least 50 questions daily - Attempt 8-10 full-length mock tests - Analyze errors thoroughly after each test - Time yourself strictly (90 minutes for 100 questions)[13][1]

    3. Revision Techniques: - Create flashcards for theories and definitions - Use mnemonics for remembering lists - Revise high-weightage topics every 3 days - Make formula sheets and key point summaries[13]

    4. Time Management: - Allocate 18 minutes per 20-question section - Don't spend more than 50 seconds per question - Attempt easier questions first - Keep 5-10 minutes for final review[13]

    5. Exam Day Strategy: - Start with your strongest section - Skip confusing questions initially - Be cautious with negative marking (avoid random guessing) - Review marked questions if time permits[3][13]
