    Chapter 29: Full-Length Practice Test – 2

    Instructions

    This is the second full-length practice test. The goal is to build on your experience from the first test, improve your timing, and identify any remaining areas of weakness.

    • Total Questions: 50
    • Time Limit: 45 Minutes
    • Marking Scheme:
      • +1 mark for each correct answer.
      • -1/3 mark for each incorrect answer.
      • 0 marks for each unattempted question.
    • Instructions:
      1. Ensure you are in a quiet environment.
      2. Use a timer and adhere strictly to the 45-minute limit.
      3. After finishing, use the Answer Key to score your test.
      4. Carefully review the Detailed Explanations for all questions. Analyze why you made mistakes.

    SECTION 1: General Aptitude & Awareness (20 Questions)

    General Intelligence & Reasoning

    1. Heart : Cardiology :: Brain : ? a) Biology b) Psychology c) Neurology d) Physiology

    2. Find the next number in the series: 1, 4, 27, 16, 125, ? a) 36 b) 216 c) 64 d) 25

    3. Find the odd one out: A. Pen, B. Paper, C. Pencil, D. Marker a) A b) B c) C d) D

    4. In a certain code, MUMBAI is written as NVNCBJ. How is CHENNAI written in that code? a) DIFOOBJ b) DIDOOBJ c) DIFOPBJ d) DIFPOBJ

    5. A is the mother of B. B is the sister of C. D is the son of C. How is A related to D? a) Mother b) Aunt c) Grandmother d) Sister

    General Awareness & Computer Knowledge

    1. What is the full form of RAM in a computer? a) Read Access Memory b) Random Access Memory c) Readily Available Memory d) Random Available Memory

    2. Which of the following is an output device? a) Mouse b) Scanner c) Microphone d) Printer

    3. The Mental Healthcare Act of India, which decriminalized suicide, was passed in: a) 1982 b) 1987 c) 2007 d) 2017

    4. The headquarters of the World Health Organization (WHO) is located in: a) New York, USA b) Geneva, Switzerland c) Paris, France d) London, UK

    5. A URL stands for: a) Universal Resource Locator b) Uniform Resource Locator c) Universal Resource Link d) Uniform Resource Link

    Quantitative Aptitude

    1. A man buys a fan for ₹1000 and sells it for ₹1200. What is his profit percentage? a) 10% b) 15% c) 20% d) 25%

    2. The sum of two numbers is 40 and their difference is 10. What is the larger number? a) 15 b) 20 c) 25 d) 30

    3. A car covers a distance of 300 km in 5 hours. What is its average speed? a) 50 km/hr b) 60 km/hr c) 70 km/hr d) 80 km/hr

    4. What is 30% of 200? a) 30 b) 40 c) 50 d) 60

    5. The price of a shirt increased from ₹400 to ₹500. What is the percentage increase? a) 10% b) 20% c) 25% d) 30%

    English Language

    1. Find the part of the sentence with an error: (A) The number of cases / (B) are increasing / (C) day by day. a) A b) B c) C d) No error

    2. Choose the correct antonym for Gregarious. a) Sociable b) Friendly c) Outgoing d) Reclusive

    3. The patient needs to ______ to the treatment plan. a) adhere b) adhear c) adheer d) adheir

    4. The idiom to cry wolf means: a) To be very brave b) To raise a false alarm c) To be very scared d) To act wild

    5. Choose the correctly spelled word. a) Questionaire b) Questionnare c) Questionnaire d) Questionair

    SECTION 2: Subject Knowledge (30 Questions)

    1. The social work method that deals with the community as the client is: a) Social Casework b) Social Group Work c) Community Organization d) Social Action

    2. According to Freud, the part of the personality that is our moral compass or conscience is the: a) Id b) Ego c) Superego d) Libido

    3. Being a "doctor" is an example of a(n): a) Ascribed status b) Achieved status c) Universal status d) Role

    4. In Piaget's theory, the stage where a child learns about the world through their senses and actions is the: a) Preoperational stage b) Concrete Operational stage c) Formal Operational stage d) Sensorimotor stage

    5. A person who is angry at their boss comes home and yells at their child. This is an example of: a) Sublimation b) Displacement c) Projection d) Reaction Formation

    6. The sociological perspective that sees society as a product of everyday, face-to-face interactions is: a) Functionalism b) Conflict Theory c) Symbolic Interactionism d) Feminism

    7. A college class is an example of a: a) Primary group b) Secondary group c) In-group d) Aggregate

    8. The inability of a young child to see a situation from another person's point of view is called: a) Object Permanence b) Conservation c) Egocentrism d) Abstract Thought

    9. A truce or compromise between two hostile groups is an example of: a) Accommodation b) Assimilation c) Cooperation d) Conflict

    10. The "C" in the ABC model of attitudes stands for: a) Consequence b) Character c) Cognition d) Conduct

    11. The tendency to conform to a group's decision, even if it is wrong, was demonstrated by: a) Milgram's obedience experiment b) Asch's conformity experiment c) Freud's case studies d) Skinner's conditioning experiments

    12. The social work principle of treating every client as a unique individual is: a) Individualization b) Acceptance c) Confidentiality d) Self-Determination

    13. Which of the following is an associative social process? a) Conflict b) Competition c) Cooperation d) All of the above

    14. The book "Social Diagnosis" (1917) laid the foundation for which social work method? a) Social Group Work b) Social Casework c) Community Organization d) Social Action

    15. Denying someone a job because of their gender is an example of: a) A stereotype b) Prejudice c) Discrimination d) A more

    16. The "O" in the "Big Five" OCEAN model of personality stands for: a) Orderliness b) Openness c) Optimism d) Obedience

    17. Which social institution is primarily responsible for providing a system of beliefs and answering questions of ultimate meaning? a) Family b) Religion c) Economy d) Government

    18. When a client starts to view their social worker as a father figure, this is an example of: a) Transference b) Counter-transference c) Empathy d) Rapport

    19. The biopsychosocial model was proposed as an alternative to the older, more limited: a) Medical model b) Social model c) Psychological model d) All of the above

    20. The "D" in the "Four D's" of abnormality that refers to behavior that violates social norms is: a) Danger b) Distress c) Dysfunction d) Deviance

    21. The main conclusion from Asch's conformity studies is that: a) People are easily controlled by authority. b) Group pressure can lead people to say things they know are untrue. c) People lose their identity in a crowd. d) Most people are independent thinkers.

    22. A politician who tries to win votes by appearing as an "average citizen" is using the ______ technique. a) Bandwagon b) Testimonial c) Plain Folks d) Fear Appeal

    23. A social security measure that is based on contributions from employees and employers is: a) Social Insurance b) Social Assistance c) A government grant d) A charity donation

    24. The principle that human rights belong to everyone, everywhere, is: a) Inalienability b) Indivisibility c) Universality d) Interdependence

    25. Norms for routine, casual interaction, like saying "please," are called: a) Mores b) Laws c) Values d) Folkways

    26. The "hidden curriculum" of a school socializes children into values like: a) Reading and writing b) Punctuality and obedience c) The school's history d) Specific job skills

    27. A professor who feels torn between their role as a teacher and their role as a researcher is experiencing: a) Role Strain b) Role Conflict c) Status Inconsistency d) Master Status

    28. Blaming immigrants for a country's economic problems is a classic example of: a) The Just-World Phenomenon b) Scapegoating c) In-group bias d) The Contact Hypothesis

    29. The stages of group development (Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing) were proposed by: a) Murray Ross b) Tuckman c) H.H. Perlman d) Mary Richmond

    30. The "M" in the NMHP of India stands for: a) Medical b) Mental c) Ministerial d) Management

    Detailed Explanations

    (Explanations for each question would be provided here. For brevity, a selection is shown.)

    • 1. (C) Neurology: Cardiology is the study of the heart; Neurology is the study of the brain and nervous system.
    • 2. (A) 36: The series alternates between cubes and squares: 1³, 2², 3³, 4², 5³. The next term is 6², which is 36.
    • 11. (C) 20%: Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price = 1200 - 1000 = 200. Profit % = (Profit / Cost Price) * 100 = (200 / 1000) * 100 = 20%.
    • 16. (B) are increasing: The subject is "The number," which is singular. "The number... is increasing." (A number of cases... are increasing).
    • 17. (D) Reclusive: Gregarious means sociable and fond of company. Reclusive means avoiding the company of other people.
    • 23. (B) Achieved status: Being a doctor is a status acquired through effort and education.
    • 25. (B) Displacement: The anger is shifted from the original target (the boss) to a safer target (the child).
    • 28. (C) Egocentrism: This is the term Piaget used for the inability to take another's perspective.
    • 29. (A) Accommodation: This is a compromise to suspend conflict, not a fusion of cultures (assimilation).
    • 38. (A) Transference: The client is transferring feelings from a past relationship (with a father) onto the therapist.
    • 43. (A) Social Insurance: Insurance is based on contributions. Assistance is based on need.
    • 47. (A) Role Strain: The tension is between different roles attached to a single status (professor). Role conflict is between two or more statuses.
    • 49. (B) Tuckman: Bruce Tuckman proposed the famous "Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing" model of group development.
