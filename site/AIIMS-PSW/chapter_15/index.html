    Chapter 15: Methods of Social Work

    Learning Objectives

    Upon completing this chapter, you will be able to: * Identify and differentiate between the primary and secondary methods of social work. * Describe the process, components, and techniques of Social Casework. * Explain the principles, stages of development, and uses of Social Group Work. * Understand the concept, principles, and models of Community Organization. * Briefly define the secondary (enabling) methods: Social Action, Social Welfare Administration, and Social Work Research.

    Exam Relevance

    • Mark Weight: Very High. This chapter details the "how-to" of the social work profession. Expect 3-4 questions on the different methods, their classification, and key concepts.
    • Topic Category: Subject Knowledge - Social Welfare & Social Work Framework.
    • Importance: Critical. A deep understanding of the methods of social work is essential for any aspiring social worker and is a core component of the syllabus.

    Detailed Theory / Concepts

    Introduction to Social Work Methods

    If social work values and principles are the "why," then the methods of social work are the "how." They are the systematic, orderly, and scientific ways in which a professional social worker intervenes to help people solve their psychosocial problems.

    The methods are broadly classified into two categories:

    1. Primary Methods (or Direct Methods): These involve direct, face-to-face contact with the client system.

      • Social Casework
      • Social Group Work
      • Community Organization

    2. Secondary Methods (or Indirect/Enabling Methods): These are methods that support the primary methods and create a better environment for practice.

      • Social Action
      • Social Welfare Administration
      • Social Work Research

    Primary Method 1: Social Casework

    • Definition: Social Casework is the method of helping individuals on a one-to-one basis to resolve their personal and social problems. The client is an individual (or a family unit treated as one).
    • Pioneer: Mary Richmond, whose 1917 book "Social Diagnosis" laid the scientific foundation for this method.
    • The Components (The 4 Ps): H.H. Perlman described casework as a process involving a Person with a Problem who comes to a Place (a social agency) where a professional representative (the caseworker) helps them by a Process.
    • The Process:
      1. Intake/Study: Gathering information about the client and their problem.
      2. Psychosocial Diagnosis/Assessment: Analyzing the information to understand the nature of the problem.
      3. Treatment/Intervention: Implementing a plan of action to solve the problem.
      4. Evaluation: Assessing the effectiveness of the intervention.
      5. Termination: Ending the professional relationship once the goals are met.

    Primary Method 2: Social Group Work

    • Definition: Social Group Work is a method where individuals are helped to improve their social functioning through purposeful group experiences. The group itself is the primary tool for change.
    • Key Idea: Individuals in a group can learn from each other, gain mutual support, and practice new social skills in a safe environment.
    • Uses: Support groups (e.g., for caregivers of Alzheimer's patients), therapeutic groups (e.g., for addiction recovery), and skill-development groups (e.g., for teaching social skills to adolescents).
    • Stages of Group Development (Tuckman's Model):
      1. Forming: The group comes together; members are polite and uncertain.
      2. Storming: Conflict arises as members assert their individuality and roles.
      3. Norming: The group establishes rules and norms; cohesion begins.
      4. Performing: The group works effectively and collaboratively towards its goals.
      5. Adjourning: The group disbands after its purpose is fulfilled.

    Primary Method 3: Community Organization

    • Definition: Community Organization is a macro-level method where the community is the client. The social worker helps the community to identify its common needs, find resources, and take collective action to address those needs.
    • Pioneer: Key contributions were made by Murray Ross.
    • Core Principles:
      • Felt Needs: The work should be based on the needs that the community itself feels are important.
      • Participation: The community members must be actively involved in the entire process.
      • Self-Help: The goal is to empower the community to solve its own problems.
    • Models of Community Organization (by Jack Rothman):
      • Locality Development: Focuses on community participation and building consensus. The process is more important than the outcome. (e.g., a neighborhood clean-up drive).
      • Social Planning: A technical, expert-driven process of problem-solving. The focus is on finding the most rational solution. (e.g., a city planning department designing a new transport system).
      • Social Action: A confrontational approach used to challenge existing power structures and bring about fundamental changes in policy. It is about shifting power to the disadvantaged. (e.g., a protest movement demanding policy change).

    The Secondary (Enabling) Methods

    1. Social Action: As described in Rothman's model, this method aims to achieve social justice by mobilizing people to challenge and change unjust systems. It involves advocacy, propaganda, boycotts, and protests. Example: The Narmada Bachao Andolan.
    2. Social Welfare Administration: This is the process of transforming social policy into social services. It involves the management of social agencies, including planning, budgeting, staffing, directing, and reporting (POSDCORB).
    3. Social Work Research: This is the systematic investigation used to build knowledge for the social work profession and to evaluate the effectiveness of its interventions. It helps in understanding social problems and finding out "what works."

    AIIMS CRE-Oriented Tips & Tricks

    • Primary vs. Secondary: This is a fundamental classification. Be able to list the three primary and three secondary methods.
    • Person-Problem-Place-Process: Associate this "4 Ps" formula with Social Casework.
    • Rothman's 3 Models: Know the names and the key focus of each model of Community Organization: Locality Development (process/cooperation), Social Planning (task/expert), Social Action (power/confrontation).
    • Keywords are Key:
      • Casework -> Individual
      • Group Work -> Group
      • Community Organization -> Community
      • Social Action -> Movement/Change
      • Administration -> Agency/Management
      • Research -> Knowledge/Evaluation

    Practice MCQs

    1. Which of the following is a Primary Method of social work? a) Social Action b) Social Work Research c) Social Group Work d) Social Welfare Administration

    2. The book "Social Diagnosis" (1917), which laid the foundation for social casework, was written by: a) H.H. Perlman b) Grace Mathew c) Mary Richmond d) Jack Rothman

    3. In which method of social work is the "group" itself considered the primary tool for change? a) Social Casework b) Social Group Work c) Community Organization d) Social Action

    4. The principle of "felt needs" is most closely associated with which method? a) Social Casework b) Social Group Work c) Community Organization d) Social Work Research

    5. Tuckman's model (Forming, Storming, Norming, Performing) describes the stages of: a) Casework process b) Group development c) Community organization d) Social action

    6. Which of the following is considered a Secondary (or enabling) Method of social work? a) Social Casework b) Social Group Work c) Community Organization d) Social Welfare Administration

    7. A social worker helping a neighborhood to demand better sanitation from the municipality is practicing: a) Social Casework b) Social Group Work c) Community Organization d) Social Work Research

    8. According to Jack Rothman, which model of community organization is confrontational and aims to shift power structures? a) Locality Development b) Social Planning c) Social Action d) Community Development

    9. The process of transforming social policy into social services within an agency is called: a) Social Action b) Social Welfare Administration c) Social Casework d) Social Legislation

    10. The "4 Ps" (Person, Problem, Place, Process) are components of which method? a) Social Casework b) Social Group Work c) Community Organization d) Social Action

    Answer Key with Explanations

    1. c) Social Group Work: Casework, Group Work, and Community Organization are the three primary methods.
    2. c) Mary Richmond: She is considered one of the principal founders of professional social casework.
    3. b) Social Group Work: In this method, the interactions and mutual support within the group are used to help individuals.
    4. c) Community Organization: This method emphasizes that intervention should be based on the needs identified by the community members themselves.
    5. b) Group development: This is a classic model describing the phases that small groups go through from formation to completion of their task.
    6. d) Social Welfare Administration: This is a secondary method that supports direct practice by ensuring agencies are well-managed.
    7. c) Community Organization: The social worker is working with the community as a whole (the client) to address a collective problem.
    8. c) Social Action: The Social Action model uses conflict and confrontation to challenge the status quo and advocate for the disadvantaged.
    9. b) Social Welfare Administration: This is the core definition of social welfare administration—the management of social services.
    10. a) Social Casework: These components were famously articulated by H.H. Perlman to describe the casework process.

    Chapter Summary (Key Takeaways)

    • The methods of social work are the professional, systematic procedures used to address psychosocial problems.
    • The Primary Methods involve direct practice: Social Casework (with individuals), Social Group Work (with groups), and Community Organization (with communities).
    • The Secondary Methods enable direct practice: Social Action (advocacy and system change), Social Welfare Administration (managing services), and Social Work Research (building knowledge).
    • Understanding the key pioneers (Mary Richmond, Murray Ross), core principles ("felt needs"), and models (Tuckman's stages, Rothman's models) associated with each method is crucial for the exam.
