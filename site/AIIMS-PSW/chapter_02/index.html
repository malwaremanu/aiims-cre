    Chapter 2: Exam Pattern, Marking Scheme & Preparation Strategy

    Learning Objectives

    Upon completing this chapter, you will be able to: * Understand the detailed structure of the AIIMS CRE for the Psychiatric Social Worker post. * Explain the marking scheme, including the policy on negative marking. * Identify the key knowledge domains covered in the syllabus for both general and subject-specific sections. * Develop a structured, phase-wise strategy for effective preparation. * Apply time management techniques for the actual examination. * Recognize the critical role of mock tests and analyzing previous year papers.

    Exam Relevance

    • Mark Weight: This chapter provides the framework for your preparation. While you won't find direct questions from this chapter in the exam, mastering its contents is essential for scoring high across all other sections.
    • Topic Category: Preparation Strategy & Exam Orientation.
    • Importance: Critical. This chapter is the strategic blueprint for your entire preparation journey. A clear understanding of the pattern and a solid strategy can be the difference between success and failure.

    Detailed Theory / Concepts

    Structure of the AIIMS CRE Exam

    The AIIMS CRE is conducted as a Computer-Based Test (CBT). The examination for the post of Psychiatric Social Worker is designed to assess a candidate's aptitude and domain knowledge comprehensively.

    • Exam Duration: 90 minutes
    • Total Questions: 100 Multiple-Choice Questions (MCQs)
    • Total Marks: 100
    • Exam Medium: English

    The exam paper is divided into two distinct parts:

    Table: AIIMS CRE Exam Structure

    Part Subject Number of MCQs Maximum Marks
    Part A General Aptitude & Awareness 40 40
    Part B Domain-Specific Knowledge 60 60
    Total 100 100

    Detailed Syllabus Breakdown

    Part A: General Section (40 Marks)

    This section is common for many posts and tests your general aptitude. It is crucial for a good rank.

    • General Intelligence & Reasoning (10 Marks): Topics include analogies, series (number, alphabet), spatial visualization, problem-solving, and logical reasoning.
    • General Awareness (10 Marks): Current affairs (national and international), basic science, Indian history and geography, and general knowledge about computers.
    • Quantitative Aptitude (10 Marks): Arithmetic, percentages, profit & loss, time & work, simple & compound interest, and data interpretation.
    • English Language & Comprehension (10 Marks): Grammar, vocabulary (synonyms, antonyms), sentence structure, and reading comprehension.

    Part B: Domain-Specific Section (60 Marks)

    This is the most critical section for a Psychiatric Social Worker post. The syllabus is designed to test your foundational and applied knowledge in subjects relevant to the role. The questions will be based on the educational qualifications required for the post, which include a Master's in Social Work or Psychology.

    Key Subject Areas: * Professional Social Work: History, philosophy, principles, methods (casework, group work, community organization), and social welfare administration. * Psychology & Mental Health: Basic psychology, developmental psychology, abnormal psychology, psychopathology, and concepts of mental health and illness (based on ICD/DSM). * Sociology & Social Psychology: Social structure, social institutions, culture, socialization, attitudes, communication, and group dynamics. * Human Rights & Social Legislation: Knowledge of key acts like the Mental Healthcare Act, 2017, Persons with Disabilities Act, and rights of vulnerable groups.

    Marking Scheme

    Understanding the marking scheme is vital for developing an effective answering strategy.

    • Correct Answer: +1 Mark
    • Incorrect Answer: -1/3 Mark (Negative Marking)
    • Unattempted Question: 0 Marks

    The presence of negative marking means that blind guessing is counterproductive. It is better to leave a question unanswered if you have no idea about the correct option.

    A Phase-Wise Preparation Strategy

    A structured approach is essential to cover the vast syllabus effectively.

    Diagram: Three-Phased Preparation (Explained in Words)

    1. Phase 1: Foundation Building (First 2 Months):

      • Goal: To gain conceptual clarity.
      • Action: Thoroughly read the standard textbooks mentioned in the reference list (e.g., Sanjay Bhattacharya for Social Work, Baron & Misra for Psychology). Make concise notes. Do not focus on MCQs yet.

    2. Phase 2: Application & Practice (Next 2 Months):

      • Goal: To apply knowledge and improve question-solving skills.
      • Action: Start solving MCQs from practice books and previous year papers (UGC-NET, other SW exams). Analyze your mistakes. This phase connects theory with application.

    3. Phase 3: Revision & Mock Tests (Last Month):

      • Goal: To enhance speed, accuracy, and exam temperament.
      • Action: Take full-length mock tests in a timed, exam-like environment. Analyze your performance in detail—identify weak areas, time taken per question, and accuracy. Revise your notes intensively.

    Real-life Examples or Case Applications

    Scenario 1: Study Plan for a Working Professional * Challenge: Limited time. * Strategy: * Dedicate 2-3 hours daily on weekdays (focus on one topic). * Utilize weekends for longer study sessions (6-8 hours), covering larger topics and practicing MCQs. * Use digital notes for quick revision during commutes or breaks. * Prioritize domain-specific subjects over general aptitude if time is short, but do not ignore the latter completely.

    Scenario 2: Study Plan for a Full-Time Student * Advantage: Ample time. * Strategy: * Create a daily schedule of 6-8 hours of focused study. * Divide the day into slots: 3 hours for domain subjects, 2 hours for the general section, and 2 hours for practice/revision. * Can afford to go into greater depth in each topic. * Should aim to complete at least 20-30 full-length mock tests.

    AIIMS CRE-Oriented Tips & Tricks

    • Accuracy over Attempt: Due to negative marking, your goal should be to maximize your score, not the number of attempts. A score of 70 with 90% accuracy is far better than a score of 80 with 70% accuracy.
    • Don't Neglect Part A: The 40 marks from the general section are often the rank-decider. Consistent, light preparation for this section from the beginning is crucial.
    • Master the Core: For Part B, focus on the core methods of social work, theories of personality, basics of psychopathology, and key social legislation. These are high-yield areas.
    • Analyze Previous Papers: Go through previous papers of AIIMS, NIMHANS, and UGC-NET (Social Work/Psychology) to understand the pattern, question style, and important topics.

    Practice MCQs

    1. What is the total duration of the AIIMS CRE for the PSW post? a) 60 minutes b) 90 minutes c) 120 minutes d) 180 minutes

    2. How many questions are there in the domain-specific section (Part B)? a) 40 b) 50 c) 60 d) 100

    3. What is the penalty for an incorrect answer in the AIIMS CRE? a) -1 mark b) -0.5 marks c) -1/3 mark d) No negative marking

    4. Which of the following subjects is part of the General Section (Part A)? a) Abnormal Psychology b) Social Case Work c) Quantitative Aptitude d) The Mental Healthcare Act

    5. The AIIMS CRE is conducted in which mode? a) Paper-Pencil Based b) Computer-Based Test (CBT) c) Interview only d) A mix of written and oral tests

    6. According to the suggested preparation strategy, when should a candidate focus on taking full-length mock tests? a) In the first month of preparation b) Throughout the preparation c) In the last month before the exam d) After the exam results are declared

    7. If a candidate attempts 80 questions, gets 65 correct and 15 incorrect, what is their final score? a) 65 b) 60 c) 50 d) 55

    8. The domain-specific syllabus for the PSW post primarily draws from the curriculum of: a) MBBS b) Master's in Social Work/Psychology c) Bachelor's in Nursing d) PhD in Sociology

    9. Which is the most advisable strategy when faced with a question you know nothing about? a) Guess one of the options b) Leave the question unattempted c) Mark the longest option d) Spend 10 minutes trying to solve it

    10. The primary goal of the "Foundation Building" phase is: a) To maximize the number of solved MCQs b) To achieve conceptual clarity c) To improve speed and accuracy d) To analyze mock test performance

    Answer Key with Explanations

    1. b) 90 minutes: The total time allotted for the 100 questions is 90 minutes.
    2. c) 60: The domain-specific section, which is critical for selection, consists of 60 questions.
    3. c) -1/3 mark: For every wrong answer, one-third of a mark is deducted from the total score.
    4. c) Quantitative Aptitude: This is a component of the 40-mark general section. The other options are part of the domain-specific syllabus.
    5. b) Computer-Based Test (CBT): The exam is conducted online at designated centers.
    6. c) In the last month before the exam: The final phase of preparation should be dedicated to intensive revision and simulating the exam experience with mock tests.
    7. b) 60: Marks for correct answers = 65 * 1 = 65. Penalty for incorrect answers = 15 * (1/3) = 5. Final score = 65 - 5 = 60.
    8. b) Master's in Social Work/Psychology: The domain-specific questions are designed to test the knowledge acquired during the qualifying master's degree.
    9. b) Leave the question unattempted: Due to negative marking, blind guessing can significantly harm your score. It is safest to skip questions you are unsure about.
    10. b) To achieve conceptual clarity: The initial phase should focus on building a strong theoretical foundation by reading standard textbooks before moving on to practice.

    Chapter Summary (Key Takeaways)

    • The AIIMS CRE is a 90-minute, 100-mark CBT with two parts: a 40-mark General section and a 60-mark Domain-Specific section.
    • There is a penalty of -1/3 mark for every incorrect answer, making accuracy crucial.
    • A structured, three-phase preparation strategy (Foundation -> Practice -> Mocks) is highly effective.
    • The domain-specific syllabus requires in-depth knowledge of Social Work, Psychology, Sociology, and relevant legislation.
    • Success depends on a balanced preparation of both parts of the syllabus, combined with smart time management and extensive practice with mock tests.
