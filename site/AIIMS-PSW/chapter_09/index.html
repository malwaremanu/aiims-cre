    Chapter 9: Vocabulary Building

    Learning Objectives

    Upon completing this chapter, you will be able to: * Appreciate the importance of a strong vocabulary for the AIIMS CRE and professional communication. * Apply effective strategies for learning and retaining new words, including the root-word method. * Confidently answer vocabulary-based questions, such as synonyms, antonyms, and one-word substitutions. * Utilize context clues to infer the meaning of unfamiliar words while reading. * Master a list of high-frequency words relevant to the exam's subject matter.

    Exam Relevance

    • Mark Weight: High. You can expect 3-4 direct questions on vocabulary (synonyms, antonyms, one-word substitution, spelling).
    • Topic Category: English Language.
    • Importance: Critical. A rich vocabulary is the key to unlocking the entire English section. It improves your reading speed and comprehension, helps you understand the nuances of questions, and allows for more precise expression.

    Detailed Theory / Concepts

    Why Build Your Vocabulary?

    Vocabulary is the collection of words you know. A larger vocabulary allows you to understand others and express yourself with greater clarity and precision. In a professional context like social work, using the right word—for instance, knowing the difference between "sympathy" and "empathy"—is crucial for effective communication.

    • Sympathy: Feeling pity or sorrow for someone else's misfortune. (Feeling for someone).
    • Empathy: The ability to understand and share the feelings of another. (Feeling with someone).

    Effective Vocabulary Building Strategies

    1. Read Widely: This is the most natural way to learn new words. Read articles, journals, and books related to social work, psychology, and sociology. Exposure to words in context helps with retention.
    2. The Root Word Method: Many English words are built from Greek and Latin roots, prefixes, and suffixes. Learning these can help you decode hundreds of words.
      • Prefix: Comes at the beginning (e.g., mal- means "bad," as in maladaptive).
      • Root: The core of the word (e.g., -path- means "feeling," as in empathy).
      • Suffix: Comes at the end (e.g., -logy means "study of," as in psychology).
    3. Keep a Word Journal: When you encounter a new word, write it down. Note its meaning, a synonym, an antonym, and use it in a sentence of your own. This active process aids memory.
    4. Use Flashcards: Write the word on one side and its meaning on the other. This is great for active recall and quick revision sessions.

    Common Vocabulary Question Types

    1. Synonyms and Antonyms

    • Synonyms: Words that have a similar meaning.
      • Example: Alleviate -> Relieve, Lessen, Mitigate.
    • Antonyms: Words that have the opposite meaning.
      • Example: Benevolent -> Malevolent, Malicious, Unkind.

    Strategy: Pay attention to the word's connotation (positive, negative, or neutral). This can help you eliminate options quickly. For example, if the word is positive, its synonym must also be positive.

    2. One-Word Substitution

    This involves replacing a descriptive phrase with a single, precise word. * Example: "A person who is recovering from an illness." -> Convalescent. * Example: "An irrational fear of open spaces." -> Agoraphobia.

    High-Frequency Word List for AIIMS CRE

    This list contains words relevant to the fields of social work, psychology, and general academic usage.

    Word Meaning Example Sentence
    Alleviate To make suffering or a problem less severe. The social worker's intervention helped to alleviate the family's distress.
    Efficacy The ability to produce a desired or intended result. The study was designed to test the efficacy of the new therapy.
    Stigma A mark of disgrace associated with a particular circumstance, quality, or person. There is still a significant stigma attached to mental illness.
    Resilience The capacity to recover quickly from difficulties; toughness. Building resilience is a key goal of therapeutic intervention.
    Cognitive Relating to the mental processes of thinking, knowing, remembering, and understanding. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) focuses on changing patterns of thinking.
    Juxtapose To place different things side by side to compare or contrast them. The report juxtaposes the patient's past and present conditions.
    Ambivalent Having mixed feelings or contradictory ideas about something or someone. The patient felt ambivalent about starting the new medication.
    Malinger To pretend to be ill in order to escape duty or work. The assessment concluded that the patient was genuinely in pain and not malingering.
    Taciturn (of a person) reserved or uncommunicative in speech; saying little. After the trauma, the child became taciturn and withdrawn.
    Ubiquitous Present, appearing, or found everywhere. In modern society, the influence of social media is ubiquitous.

    AIIMS CRE-Oriented Tips & Tricks

    • Eliminate Options: If you don't know the meaning of the word in the question, look at the options. Can you eliminate any that are clearly unrelated or have the wrong connotation?
    • Break Down the Word: Use the root-word method on the fly. If you see a word like "anachronism," you might recognize ana- (against) and chron- (time) and infer that it means something out of its proper time.
    • Context is King: If a word is in a sentence, the surrounding words provide valuable clues to its meaning.

    Practice MCQs

    2. Aberration a) Deviation b) Normalcy c) Abundance d) Justification

    3. Cogent a) Weak b) Confusing c) Convincing d) Complicated

    4. Esoteric a) Widely known b) Simple c) Foreign d) Obscure

    5. Proscribe a) Recommend b) Forbid c) Announce d) Analyze

    Antonyms (Choose the word with the opposite meaning)

    1. Gregarious a) Sociable b) Loud c) Introverted d) Angry

    2. Mitigate a) Lessen b) Worsen c) Postpone d) Allow

    3. Furtive a) Secretive b) Open c) Quick d) Lazy

    4. Laudable a) Praiseworthy b) Contemptible c) Laughable d) Capable

    5. Meticulous a) Careful b) Hasty c) Messy d) Careless

    One-Word Substitution

    1. A person who is new to a profession or activity. a) Veteran b) Novice c) Expert d) Virtuoso

    2. The study of the mind and behavior. a) Sociology b) Anthropology c) Psychology d) Physiology

    3. A remedy for all diseases. a) Panacea b) Antidote c) Antibiotic d) Toxin

    4. Lasting for a very short time. a) Eternal b) Ephemeral c) Perpetual d) Enduring

    5. A person who is indifferent to pain and pleasure. a) Stoic b) Hedonist c) Ascetic d) Egoist

    Answer Key with Explanations

    1. b) Practical: Empirical evidence is based on observation and experience (practice), not just on theory.
    2. a) Deviation: An aberration is a departure from what is normal, usual, or expected—a deviation.
    3. c) Convincing: A cogent argument is clear, logical, and convincing.
    4. d) Obscure: Esoteric knowledge is intended for or likely to be understood by only a small number of people with a specialized knowledge, hence it is obscure.
    5. b) Forbid: To proscribe something is to formally forbid it by law or rule.
    6. c) Introverted: Gregarious means fond of company; sociable. The opposite is someone who is shy or introverted.
    7. b) Worsen: To mitigate is to make something less severe. The opposite is to worsen or aggravate it.
    8. b) Open: Furtive means attempting to avoid notice or attention, typically because of guilt; secretive. The opposite is being open and transparent.
    9. b) Contemptible: Laudable means deserving praise. The opposite is contemptible, meaning deserving contempt or scorn.
    10. d) Careless: Meticulous means showing great attention to detail; very careful and precise. The direct opposite is being careless. While "hasty" and "messy" can be results of carelessness, "careless" is the most precise antonym.
    11. b) Novice: A novice is a beginner. A veteran or expert is experienced. A virtuoso is a master.
    12. c) Psychology: This is the definition of the field of psychology.
    13. a) Panacea: A panacea is a solution or remedy for all difficulties or diseases.
    14. b) Ephemeral: This word specifically means lasting for a very short time.
    15. a) Stoic: A stoic is a person who can endure pain or hardship without showing their feelings or complaining.

    Chapter Summary (Key Takeaways)

    • A strong vocabulary is essential for all parts of the English section and for professional clarity.
    • Active learning strategies like using the root-word method and keeping a word journal are more effective than passive reading.
    • For vocabulary questions, focus on the word's connotation and use the process of elimination to narrow down the options.
    • Learning a core list of high-frequency academic and subject-relevant words will provide a significant advantage.
    • Practice is key to making new words a permanent part of your vocabulary.
