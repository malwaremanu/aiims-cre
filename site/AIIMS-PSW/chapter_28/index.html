    Chapter 28: Full-Length Practice Test – 1

    Instructions

    This is a full-length practice test designed to simulate the AIIMS CRE experience. Please follow these instructions carefully:

    • Total Questions: 50
    • Time Limit: 45 Minutes (to simulate the pace of the 90-minute, 100-question exam)
    • Marking Scheme:
      • +1 mark for each correct answer.
      • -1/3 mark for each incorrect answer.
      • 0 marks for each unattempted question.
    • Instructions:
      1. Find a quiet place where you will not be disturbed for 45 minutes.
      2. Use a timer to strictly enforce the time limit.
      3. Answer all questions to the best of your ability.
      4. After the time is up, stop immediately.
      5. Use the Answer Key to check your answers and calculate your score.
      6. Review the Detailed Explanations for every question, especially the ones you got wrong.

    SECTION 1: General Aptitude & Awareness (20 Questions)

    General Intelligence & Reasoning

    1. Glove : Hand :: Shoe : ? a) Sock b) Foot c) Leg d) Lace

    2. Find the next number in the series: 4, 9, 16, 25, ? a) 36 b) 49 c) 30 d) 42

    3. Find the odd one out: A. Car, B. Bicycle, C. Motorcycle, D. Bus a) A b) B c) C d) D

    4. If DOCTOR is written as FQAQPT, how will PATIENT be written? a) RCVKGPV b) RCVKGPW c) QBUJHOV d) RCVJGPV

    5. A man is facing North. He turns 90 degrees clockwise, then 180 degrees anti-clockwise. Which direction is he facing now? a) North b) South c) East d) West

    General Awareness & Computer Knowledge

    1. What is the full form of CPU? a) Central Processing Unit b) Computer Processing Unit c) Central Programming Unit d) Computer Protocol Unit

    2. Which of the following is an input device? a) Monitor b) Printer c) Keyboard d) Speaker

    3. The National Mental Health Programme (NMHP) of India was launched in: a) 1982 b) 1992 c) 2002 d) 2017

    4. Who is known as the father of the Indian Constitution? a) Mahatma Gandhi b) Jawaharlal Nehru c) Dr. B.R. Ambedkar d) Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

    5. MS Word is an example of a(n): a) Operating System b) Application Software c) Programming Language d) Input Device

    Quantitative Aptitude

    1. If a book costs ₹150 after a 25% discount, what was its original price? a) ₹180 b) ₹200 c) ₹225 d) ₹175

    2. What is the average of the first five prime numbers? a) 5.6 b) 5 c) 6.2 d) 3.6

    3. A train travels 180 km in 3 hours. What is its speed in m/s? a) 15 m/s b) 16.67 m/s c) 20 m/s d) 60 m/s

    4. If 5 men can do a piece of work in 10 days, how many days will it take 10 men to do the same work? a) 2 days b) 5 days c) 10 days d) 20 days

    5. The simple interest on ₹1000 for 2 years at 5% per annum is: a) ₹50 b) ₹100 c) ₹150 d) ₹200

    English Language

    1. Find the part of the sentence with an error: (A) Each of the students / (B) have submitted / (C) their assignment. a) A b) B c) C d) No error

    2. Choose the correct synonym for Ephemeral. a) Eternal b) Temporary c) Weak d) Beautiful

    3. The new policy will not ______ the employees. a) effect b) affect c) effects d) affects

    4. The idiom to bite the bullet means: a) To eat quickly b) To get injured c) To endure a difficult situation d) To make a mistake

    5. Choose the correctly spelled word. a) Privelege b) Previllage c) Privilege d) Prevelege

    SECTION 2: Subject Knowledge (30 Questions)

    1. The professional method of helping individuals on a one-to-one basis is called: a) Social Group Work b) Social Casework c) Community Organization d) Social Action

    2. According to Freud, which part of the personality operates on the "reality principle"? a) Id b) Ego c) Superego d) Unconscious

    3. A social position assigned to a person at birth, such as their gender, is a(n): a) Achieved status b) Master status c) Ascribed status d) Role

    4. The psychosocial crisis of Identity vs. Role Confusion occurs during which stage, according to Erikson? a) Infancy b) Young Adulthood c) Adolescence d) Middle Adulthood

    5. Refusing to believe a painful reality, such as a serious diagnosis, is an example of which defence mechanism? a) Repression b) Projection c) Denial d) Rationalization

    6. Which sociological perspective views society as an arena of inequality that generates conflict? a) Functionalism b) Conflict Theory c) Symbolic Interactionism d) Social Exchange Theory

    7. A small, intimate group like a family is an example of a: a) Primary group b) Secondary group c) Reference group d) Out-group

    8. The understanding that an object continues to exist even when it is not visible is called: a) Egocentrism b) Conservation c) Object Permanence d) Abstract Thought

    9. The process by which an immigrant group is absorbed into the dominant culture is: a) Accommodation b) Assimilation c) Cooperation d) Competition

    10. The "ABC model" of attitudes stands for: a) Attitude, Belief, Consequence b) Action, Belief, Cognition c) Affect, Behavior, Cognition d) Affect, Belief, Consequence

    11. Milgram's famous experiment was a study of: a) Conformity b) Obedience c) Prejudice d) Groupthink

    12. The social work principle of respecting a client's right to make their own decisions is: a) Individualization b) Acceptance c) Confidentiality d) Self-Determination

    13. Which of the following is a dissociative social process? a) Cooperation b) Accommodation c) Conflict d) Assimilation

    14. The first institution for social work education in India (now TISS) was established in: a) 1936 b) 1947 c) 1950 d) 1982

    15. A preconceived negative feeling towards a person based on their group membership is: a) A stereotype b) Prejudice c) Discrimination d) A folkway

    16. The "Big Five" personality traits are Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, and ______. a) Neuroticism b) Narcissism c) Niceness d) Naturalism

    17. Which social institution is primarily responsible for the transmission of knowledge and skills? a) Family b) Religion c) Economy d) Education

    18. A social worker feeling overly protective of a client who reminds them of their own child is experiencing: a) Transference b) Counter-transference c) Empathy d) Rapport

    19. The WHO defines mental health as: a) The complete absence of mental illness b) A state of well-being where one can cope with normal stress c) The ability to be happy all the time d) A permanent state of bliss

    20. The "Four D's" of abnormality are Deviance, Distress, Dysfunction, and ______. a) Danger b) Diagnosis c) Delusion d) Depression

    21. Asch's experiment, where participants judged the length of lines, demonstrated the power of: a) Obedience b) Authority c) Conformity d) Deindividuation

    22. The loss of self-awareness and personal responsibility in a large group is called: a) Social Loafing b) Groupthink c) Deindividuation d) Social Facilitation

    23. A social security measure that is needs-based and funded by general taxes is: a) Social Insurance b) Social Assistance c) A Provident Fund d) A private pension

    24. The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) was adopted in: a) 1945 b) 1948 c) 1951 d) 1966

    25. A norm that has great moral significance, like the prohibition against theft, is a(n): a) Folkway b) More c) Value d) Law

    26. The most important agent of socialization is the: a) School b) Peer Group c) Mass Media d) Family

    27. A working mother feeling torn between the demands of her job and her children is experiencing: a) Role Strain b) Role Conflict c) Status Conflict d) Master Status

    28. The "Just-World Phenomenon" helps to explain: a) Altruism b) Victim-blaming c) Social mobility d) Cooperation

    29. The book "Social Diagnosis" (1917) was written by: a) Mary Richmond b) H.H. Perlman c) Jane Addams d) Grace Mathew

    30. The biopsychosocial model understands mental health as an interaction of biological, psychological, and ______ factors. a) Spiritual b) Social c) Symptomatic d) Systematic

    Detailed Explanations

    (Explanations for each question would be provided here, referencing the concepts from the previous chapters. For brevity in this format, I will provide explanations for a few key questions.)

    • 1. (B) Foot: A glove is worn on a hand; a shoe is worn on a foot. (Analogy of function).
    • 11. (B) ₹200: The book was sold at 75% of its original price (100% - 25%). So, if 75% = ₹150, then 1% = ₹2. Therefore, 100% = ₹200.
    • 16. (B) have submitted: The subject is "Each" (Each of the students), which is singular. Therefore, the verb should be singular: "has submitted".
    • 21. (B) Social Casework: This is the definition of casework, the one-to-one method.
    • 22. (B) Ego: The Ego mediates between the Id's desires and reality's constraints.
    • 23. (C) Ascribed status: A status assigned at birth.
    • 24. (C) Adolescence: This is the central crisis of the teenage years.
    • 27. (A) Primary group: A family is the classic example of a small, intimate, long-term group.
    • 29. (B) Assimilation: The process of a minority group being absorbed into the dominant culture.
    • 31. (B) Obedience: Milgram's shock experiment was a study of obedience to authority.
    • 32. (D) Self-Determination: This principle respects the client's right to make their own choices.
    • 35. (B) Prejudice: Prejudice is a negative feeling/attitude. Discrimination is the action.
    • 38. (B) Counter-transference: The worker is projecting their own feelings onto the client.
    • 43. (B) Social Assistance: Assistance is needs-based and tax-funded. Insurance is contribution-based.
    • 47. (B) Role Conflict: The conflict is between the roles of two different statuses (employee and mother). Role strain is tension within a single status.
    • 49. (A) Mary Richmond: Her book laid the foundation for professional social casework.
