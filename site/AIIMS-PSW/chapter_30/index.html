    Chapter 30: Full-Length Practice Test – 3

    Instructions

    This is your final practice test. Use this opportunity to consolidate all your learning, perfect your time management, and build confidence for the actual examination.

    • Total Questions: 50
    • Time Limit: 45 Minutes
    • Marking Scheme:
      • +1 mark for each correct answer.
      • -1/3 mark for each incorrect answer.
      • 0 marks for each unattempted question.
    • Instructions:
      1. Simulate exam conditions as closely as possible.
      2. Time yourself strictly.
      3. After completion, score your test using the Answer Key.
      4. Dedicate significant time to reviewing the Detailed Explanations. Understand the logic behind each answer. Good luck!

    SECTION 1: General Aptitude & Awareness (20 Questions)

    General Intelligence & Reasoning

    1. Author : Book :: Sculptor : ? a) Museum b) Stone c) Chisel d) Statue

    2. Find the next number in the series: 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, ? a) 40 b) 42 c) 44 d) 46

    3. Find the odd one out: A. Triangle, B. Square, C. Circle, D. Rectangle a) A b) B c) C d) D

    4. If WATER is coded as XBUFS, what is the code for FIRE? a) GJSF b) GJSG c) GHSF d) GJRF

    5. Pointing to a man, a woman said, "His mother is the only daughter of my mother." How is the woman related to the man? a) Mother b) Grandmother c) Sister d) Daughter

    General Awareness & Computer Knowledge

    1. Which part of the computer is known as its "brain"? a) RAM b) Monitor c) CPU d) Hard Disk

    2. The ".com" in a website address usually refers to: a) Educational b) Government c) Commercial d) Network

    3. The Protection of Human Rights Act in India was passed in: a) 1948 b) 1982 c) 1993 d) 2017

    4. Which of the following is a major social reform movement from 19th century India? a) The Green Revolution b) The White Revolution c) The Abolition of Sati d) The Narmada Bachao Andolan

    5. A computer virus is a type of: a) Hardware b) Malware c) Freeware d) Network device

    Quantitative Aptitude

    1. If the ratio of two numbers is 3:4 and their sum is 63, what is the smaller number? a) 9 b) 18 c) 27 d) 36

    2. A shopkeeper sells an item for ₹600 that he bought for ₹500. What is his profit? a) ₹50 b) ₹100 c) ₹150 d) ₹200

    3. How long will it take a train 100 meters long to cross a pole if its speed is 36 km/hr? a) 5 seconds b) 10 seconds c) 15 seconds d) 20 seconds

    4. If x = 5, what is the value of 2x² + 3x - 10? a) 50 b) 55 c) 60 d) 65

    5. A class has 20 boys and 30 girls. What percentage of the class is boys? a) 20% b) 30% c) 40% d) 50%

    English Language

    1. Find the part of the sentence with an error: (A) The furniture in this room / (B) are made / (C) of teak. a) A b) B c) C d) No error

    2. Choose the correct antonym for Taciturn. a) Reserved b) Silent c) Talkative d) Shy

    3. The committee could not arrive at a ______ decision. a) unanimous b) unannimous c) unanymous d) unanimmous

    4. The idiom to burn the midnight oil means: a) To waste time b) To work late into the night c) To start a fire d) To create problems

    5. I need your ______ on this matter. a) advise b) advice c) advize d) advyce

    SECTION 2: Subject Knowledge (30 Questions)

    1. The social work method that focuses on advocacy and challenging unjust social structures is: a) Social Casework b) Social Group Work c) Social Action d) Social Work Research

    2. A person who feels guilty about their own aggressive thoughts constantly accuses others of being aggressive. This is an example of: a) Projection b) Displacement c) Rationalization d) Sublimation

    3. A person's race is an example of a(n): a) Achieved status b) Master status c) Ascribed status d) Role

    4. According to Erikson, the main psychosocial crisis of young adulthood is: a) Trust vs. Mistrust b) Identity vs. Role Confusion c) Intimacy vs. Isolation d) Generativity vs. Stagnation

    5. Channeling unacceptable aggressive impulses into playing sports is a classic example of: a) Repression b) Denial c) Reaction Formation d) Sublimation

    6. The sociological perspective that focuses on how people use symbols to create social reality is: a) Functionalism b) Conflict Theory c) Symbolic Interactionism d) Postmodernism

    7. A group of people you admire and use to evaluate your own behavior is a(n): a) Primary group b) Secondary group c) Reference group d) Out-group

    8. According to Piaget, the ability to think logically about concrete events first emerges in the ______ stage. a) Sensorimotor b) Preoperational c) Concrete Operational d) Formal Operational

    9. Two rival companies agreeing to a price freeze to avoid a price war is an example of: a) Accommodation b) Assimilation c) Cooperation d) Conflict

    10. The belief that "spiders are creepy" is which component of an attitude? a) Affective b) Behavioral c) Cognitive d) Both A and C

    11. The powerful influence of obedience to authority was dramatically demonstrated in: a) Asch's conformity experiment b) Milgram's obedience experiment c) The Stanford Prison Experiment d) Pavlov's conditioning experiment

    12. The social work principle of "Acceptance" means: a) Agreeing with everything the client says. b) Approving of all the client's actions. c) Accepting the client as a person of inherent worth. d) Accepting that the client cannot change.

    13. Which of the following is NOT a primary method of social work? a) Social Casework b) Social Group Work c) Community Organization d) Social Work Research

    14. The process of managing a social service agency, including budgeting and staffing, is: a) Social Action b) Social Welfare Administration c) Community Organization d) Social Planning

    15. A professor who believes all students are lazy is demonstrating a(n): a) Prejudice b) Stereotype c) Discrimination d) More

    16. The "C" in the "Big Five" OCEAN model of personality stands for: a) Calmness b) Creativity c) Conscientiousness d) Courage

    17. Which social institution's primary function is to maintain social order? a) Family b) Religion c) Economy d) Government

    18. The "Four D's" of abnormality are used to: a) Classify different types of personalities. b) Determine if a behavior is clinically significant. c) Describe the stages of group development. d) List the methods of social work.

    19. The National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) of India is a(n): a) Constitutional body b) Statutory body c) Executive body d) NGO

    20. The "mean world syndrome," a concept from Cultivation Theory, suggests that: a) The world is getting worse every year. b) Heavy TV viewers may see the world as more dangerous than it is. c) People are meaner on social media than in real life. d) The media sets the agenda for public discussion.

    21. A person who is secretly jealous of a friend but acts overly supportive and happy for their success is using: a) Projection b) Displacement c) Reaction Formation d) Rationalization

    22. The feeling of anonymity in a large crowd can lead to: a) Deindividuation b) Social facilitation c) Groupthink d) Conformity

    23. The Employees' State Insurance (ESI) scheme in India is an example of: a) Social Insurance b) Social Assistance c) Social Service d) A charity

    24. The principle that all human rights are interconnected and equally important is: a) Universality b) Inalienability c) Indivisibility d) Accountability

    25. The rules for routine, casual interaction in a society are called: a) Mores b) Laws c) Folkways d) Values

    26. The "hidden curriculum" is taught by which agent of socialization? a) Family b) Peer Group c) School d) Mass Media

    27. A person's gender is a(n) __ status, while being a "CEO" is a(n) ____ status. a) achieved; ascribed b) ascribed; achieved c) master; primary d) primary; secondary

    28. The Contact Hypothesis states that prejudice can be reduced through intergroup contact under certain conditions, such as: a) Competition for resources b) Unequal status c) Common goals d) Casual, unstructured meetings

    29. The "4 Ps" (Person, Problem, Place, Process) are associated with which method? a) Social Group Work b) Community Organization c) Social Casework d) Social Action

    30. The modern, rights-based act governing mental health in India is the: a) Mental Health Act, 1987 b) Indian Lunacy Act, 1912 c) Mental Healthcare Act, 2017 d) National Trust Act, 1999

    Detailed Explanations

    (Explanations for each question would be provided here. For brevity, a selection is shown.)

    • 1. (D) Statue: An author creates a book; a sculptor creates a statue. (Creator and Creation relationship).
    • 2. (B) 42: The pattern is n² + n. (1²+1=2, 2²+2=6, 3²+3=12, 4²+4=20, 5²+5=30). The next term is 6²+6 = 42.
    • 3. (C) Circle: The triangle, square, and rectangle are polygons (made of straight lines). The circle is not.
    • 5. (A) Mother: "My mother's only daughter" is the woman herself. "His mother is me" means the woman is the man's mother.
    • 11. (C) 27: The ratio is 3:4. The sum of the parts is 3+4=7. Divide the total sum by this: 63/7 = 9. The smaller number is 3 * 9 = 27.
    • 13. (B) 10 seconds: First, convert speed to m/s: 36 km/hr = 36 * (5/18) m/s = 10 m/s. Time = Distance / Speed = 100m / 10 m/s = 10 seconds.
    • 16. (B) are made: The subject is "furniture," which is a singular, uncountable noun. The verb should be singular: "is made".
    • 20. (B) advice: "Advice" (with a 'c') is the noun (a recommendation). "Advise" (with an 's') is the verb (to give a recommendation). The sentence requires the noun.
    • 22. (A) Projection: The person is attributing their own unacceptable thoughts (aggression) to others.
    • 25. (D) Sublimation: This is the mature defence mechanism of channeling unacceptable impulses into socially valued activities.
    • 41. (C) Reaction Formation: The person is behaving in a way (overly supportive) that is the exact opposite of their true, unacceptable feeling (jealousy).
    • 47. (B) ascribed; achieved: Gender is an ascribed (involuntary) status; being a CEO is an achieved (earned) status.
    • 49. (C) Social Casework: These "4 Ps" were articulated by H.H. Perlman to describe the components of casework.
    • 50. (C) Mental Healthcare Act, 2017: This is the current, rights-based legislation.
